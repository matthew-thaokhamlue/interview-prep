## Purpose of This Stage

The team interview tests:
- Will you collaborate well with peers? (collaboration)
- Do you fit the team culture? (culture fit)
- Are you someone they'd want to work with daily? (likability)
- Do you have the skills to contribute? (competence check from peer view)
- Will you add to or disrupt team dynamics? (team chemistry)

**Interviewer Profile:** Potential peers, cross-functional partners, and team members. Each has their own lens - engineering, design, product, ops, etc.

**Success Criteria:** Team members would enjoy working with you, see you as capable, and advocate for your hire.

## Team Interview Formats

### Common Formats

| Format | Duration | What to Expect |
|--------|----------|----------------|
| **Panel interview** | 60-90 min | Multiple interviewers at once, rotating questions |
| **Sequential 1:1s** | 30-45 min each | Back-to-back meetings with different people |
| **Group session** | 60-90 min | Informal discussion, may include lunch |
| **Working session** | 60-120 min | Collaborate on a real problem together |
| **Virtual meet-the-team** | 45-60 min | Video call with team members |

### Format-Specific Tips

| Format | Key Tips |
|--------|----------|
| **Panel** | Make eye contact with all, address by name, manage multiple perspectives |
| **Sequential** | Vary your examples, stay energized, note what each person cares about |
| **Group session** | Be conversational, include everyone, show social awareness |
| **Working session** | Think aloud, ask questions, collaborate genuinely |
| **Virtual** | Check tech early, engage camera, acknowledge each person |

## 9. Questions to Ask Each Interviewer

### For Engineers/Technical Folks
1. "What's your tech stack and how do you make technology decisions?"
2. "How do [role] and engineering typically collaborate here?"
3. "What makes a [role] effective to work with from your perspective?"

### For Designers
1. "How does the design process work here?"
2. "What does good collaboration between [role] and design look like?"
3. "How do you handle trade-offs between ideal design and constraints?"

### For Operations/Other Functions
1. "What are the biggest operational challenges right now?"
2. "How does [role] typically support your team's goals?"
3. "What would make someone successful in this role from your perspective?"

### Universal Good Questions
- "What do you enjoy most about working here?"
- "What's a challenge your team is working through?"
- "What would you want to see from someone in this role in the first 90 days?"

## 11. Red Flags to Avoid

| Don't Do | Why It's Risky | Do Instead |
|----------|---------------|------------|
| Favor one interviewer | Others feel excluded | Distribute attention |
| Criticize their current approach | They might have built it | Ask curious questions |
| Be too dominant in group settings | Signals poor collaboration | Make space for others |
| Name-drop excessively | Comes across as insecure | Let work speak |
| Seem disinterested in some functions | Shows bias | Value each perspective |
| Badmouth former colleagues | Red flag for any role | Focus on positive learnings |
| Pretend to know what you don't | Will get caught | Admit gaps, show curiosity |

### Panel-Specific Tips
- Address people by name
- If asked by one person, answer to the group
- Make eye contact with each person
- Don't let one person dominate your attention

## Handling Different Interviewer Types

### The Skeptic
- **Signs:** Challenging questions, pushing back
- **Strategy:** Stay calm, use evidence, acknowledge their concerns
- **Response:** "That's a fair concern. Here's how I've addressed that before..."

### The Enthusiast
- **Signs:** Nodding, engaged, positive energy
- **Strategy:** Match energy but stay grounded
- **Response:** Build on their enthusiasm with concrete examples

### The Silent Observer
- **Signs:** Taking notes, not asking much
- **Strategy:** Include them, make eye contact, ask their opinion
- **Response:** "I'd love to hear your perspective on that, [Name]"

### The Domain Expert
- **Signs:** Deep technical questions in their area
- **Strategy:** Be honest about your knowledge level, show learning ability
- **Response:** "I have experience with [X]. For [Y], I'd collaborate closely with experts like you"

### The Culture Guardian
- **Signs:** Values-focused questions, behavioral scenarios
- **Strategy:** Authentically connect to company values
- **Response:** Reference values naturally, with genuine examples

## Team Interview Tips

### Before the Interview

- [ ] Research every interviewer on LinkedIn
- [ ] Understand each person's function and likely concerns
- [ ] Prepare function-specific questions
- [ ] Have 4-5 collaboration stories ready
- [ ] Know the company values by heart
- [ ] Plan energy management for long interviews

### During the Interview

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Use names frequently | Forget who's who |
| Distribute attention evenly | Favor one person |
| Show genuine curiosity about each function | Be dismissive of any role |
| Admit when you don't know something | Pretend expertise you lack |
| Be conversational and warm | Be stiff and formal |
| Ask about team challenges | Only ask positive questions |
| Show enthusiasm for collaboration | Focus only on your own work |

### After Each Conversation

- Note key points each person mentioned
- Note any concerns they raised
- Note what seemed to resonate
- Prepare follow-up if doing sequential interviews
