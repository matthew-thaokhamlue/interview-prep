## Purpose of This Stage

The hiring manager round tests:
- Can you actually do the job? (competence)
- Will you fit with the team? (collaboration)
- Are you coachable? (growth mindset)
- How do you handle challenges? (resilience)
- What's your working style? (day-to-day fit)

**Interviewer Profile:** Your potential direct manager. Evaluates job fit, team dynamics, working style, and whether they'd want to work with you daily.

**Success Criteria:** Manager confident you can do the job, sees you fitting with the team, wants to advocate for you.

---

## Key Differences from HR Round

| HR Round | Hiring Manager Round |
|----------|---------------------|
| Surface-level fit check | Deep behavioral assessment |
| Motivation & logistics | Competence & collaboration |
| Generic questions | Role-specific scenarios |
| 30-60 minutes | 45-90 minutes |
| Pass/fail screening | Nuanced evaluation |

## 7. Questions to Ask Hiring Manager

### Priority Questions (Ask These)
1. "What does success look like in this role at 6 months? 1 year?"
2. "What are the biggest challenges the team is facing right now?"
3. "How would you describe your management style?"
4. "What's the team structure and who would I work with most closely?"
5. "What happened to the previous person in this role?" (if applicable)

### Backup Questions
- "What do you enjoy most about working here?"
- "How does this role contribute to the company's goals?"
- "What's the team culture like?"
- "Are there opportunities for growth and development?"

### Questions That Show You've Researched
- "I noticed [company initiative]. How does this team contribute to that?"
- "Given [recent news/change], how is the team adapting?"

---

## 8. Red Flags to Avoid

| Don't Say | Why It's Bad | Say Instead |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| "I don't have weaknesses" | Lacks self-awareness | Acknowledge real growth areas |
| Badmouth previous manager | Red flag for attitude | Focus on what you learned |
| "I work too hard" | Cliché, not believable | Give genuine weakness |
| Generic answers | Shows no preparation | Use specific examples |
| "I'm not sure" (repeatedly) | Shows uncertainty | "Let me think about that..." |
| Interrupt the interviewer | Shows poor listening | Let them finish, then respond |

### Body Language
- Maintain eye contact (natural, not staring)
- Lean slightly forward (engaged)
- Avoid crossing arms (closed off)
- Mirror their energy level

## The STAR+ Method (Extended)

### Breaking Down Each Component

| Component | Time Allocation | What to Include |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Situation** | 15% | Context, stakes, why it mattered |
| **Task** | 10% | Your specific responsibility |
| **Action** | 50% | Step-by-step what YOU did |
| **Result** | 20% | Quantified outcome, impact |
| **+Learning** | 5% | Reflection, what you'd do differently |

### Action Section Tips
- Use "I" not "we" (show YOUR contribution)
- Be specific: "I analyzed..." not "We looked at..."
- Show your thought process
- Include obstacles you overcame
- Demonstrate relevant skills

### Quantifying Results
| Vague | Specific |
|-------|----------|
| "Improved sales" | "Increased sales by 25% ($500K)" |
| "Saved time" | "Reduced process time from 3 days to 4 hours" |
| "Better satisfaction" | "NPS improved from 32 to 67" |

## Hiring Manager Round Tips

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Use specific examples | Give generic answers |
| Show enthusiasm for the role | Appear disinterested |
| Ask thoughtful questions | Have no questions |
| Be concise (2-3 min per answer) | Ramble (5+ min answers) |
| Admit what you don't know | Pretend you know everything |
| Connect answers to their needs | Make it all about you |
| Show you want to learn | Act like you know it all |

## Reading the Hiring Manager

### Engagement Signals (Good)
- Nodding, leaning forward
- Taking notes
- Follow-up questions
- Sharing team context
- Mentioning "when you join..."

### Disengagement Signals (Adjust)
- Looking at clock/phone
- Short responses to your questions
- Rushing through questions
- Not making eye contact

### Adjusting Mid-Interview
- If they seem rushed: tighten your answers
- If they want depth: expand with more detail
- If they're skeptical: add more evidence
- If they're engaged: let the conversation flow
