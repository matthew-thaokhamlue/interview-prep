# Interview Day Execution

> Practical guidance for the day of the interview.

**Use:** Review before each interview
**Purpose:** Execution checklist and real-time tips

---

## Before Each Interview

- [ ] Review stage-specific prep doc
- [ ] Check interviewer LinkedIn for updates
- [ ] Prepare 3-5 questions to ask
- [ ] Test tech setup (if video)
- [ ] Have water, pen, notepad ready
- [ ] Review company's latest news

---

## During the Interview

- Listen fully before responding
- Ask clarifying questions
- Use structured answers (situation → action → result)
- Tie answers back to their needs
- Take brief notes on key points
- Watch for time cues

---

## Closing Strong

- Summarize your interest and fit
- Ask about next steps and timeline
- Thank them for their time
- Get contact info for follow-up

---

## After Each Interview

- [ ] Send thank-you email within 24 hours
- [ ] Update prep docs with new intel
- [ ] Note questions asked (pattern recognition)
- [ ] Refine stories based on what landed
- [ ] Prepare for likely follow-up topics

## Quick Reference: What Each Stage Tests

| Stage | They're Asking | You're Proving |
|-------|----------------|----------------|
| HR/Screening | "Should we invest interview time?" | Basics check out, motivated, professional |
| Hiring Manager | "Can they do the job? Will they fit?" | Competence, collaboration, coachability |
| Technical/Case | "Do they actually have the skills?" | Depth, problem-solving, honesty about gaps |
| Team Interview | "Will they work well with us?" | Collaboration, culture fit, team chemistry |
| Executive/Final | "Are they a long-term bet?" | Strategic thinking, culture, leadership |

## Video Interview Tips

### Tech Setup
- Test camera, mic, and internet 30 min before
- Use wired connection if possible
- Close unnecessary apps
- Have backup plan (phone hotspot, dial-in number)

### Environment
- Clean, neutral background
- Good lighting (face the light source)
- Minimize background noise
- Eye level camera position

### Presence
- Look at camera, not screen, when speaking
- Nod and react visibly (video flattens expressions)
- Pause slightly before responding (avoid cutting off)
- Keep gestures in frame

---

## In-Person Interview Tips

### Logistics
- Arrive 10-15 minutes early
- Know the exact building/room
- Bring printed materials (resume, presentation)
- Have interviewer contact info for emergencies

### First Impressions
- Firm handshake, eye contact
- Thank them for their time
- Match energy level to company culture
- Bring a notepad (shows preparation)

### Body Language
- Lean slightly forward (engaged)
- Open posture (don't cross arms)
- Mirror interviewer's energy
- Smile naturally

---

## Handling Difficult Moments

### If You Don't Know the Answer
> "That's a great question. I don't have direct experience with [X], but here's how I'd approach learning it..." or "Let me think through that..."

### If You Need Clarification
> "Could you help me understand what aspect you'd like me to focus on?" or "Just to make sure I address the right thing..."

### If You Made a Mistake
> "Actually, let me rephrase that..." (brief correction, don't dwell)

### If Time is Running Short
> "I want to be respectful of your time. Would you like me to summarize, or shall I continue with detail?"
